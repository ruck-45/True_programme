William Shakespeare, often regarded as the greatest playwright in the English language, lived during the late 16th and early 17th centuries. Born in Stratford-upon-Avon, England, in 1564, Shakespeare's prolific and timeless body of work includes 39 plays, 154 sonnets, and two long narrative poems. His plays, such as "Hamlet," "Romeo and Juliet," and "Macbeth," explore profound themes of love, power, betrayal, and the complexities of the human condition. Shakespeare's mastery of language, poetic expression, and deep insight into human nature have left an indelible mark on literature and theater. His works continue to be performed globally, celebrated for their universal relevance and enduring impact on the arts and culture.
